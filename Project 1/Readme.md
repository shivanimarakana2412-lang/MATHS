# 📘 Part A: Theoretical Concepts

## 🔹 1. Types of Data

### Numerical Data

Data that can be measured and expressed in numbers.
**Examples:** Age, Income, Family Size

### Categorical Data

Data that represents categories or labels.
**Examples:** Education Level, Owns House, Urban/Rural

---

## 🔹 2. Descriptive vs Inferential Statistics

### Descriptive Statistics

Summarizes and describes the dataset.
**Examples:** Mean, Median, Charts

### Inferential Statistics

Makes predictions or generalizations about a population based on sample data.

---

## - Descriptive Statistics

It involves organizing, summarizing, and presenting data using numerical measures and graphs.

---

## 🔹 2. Measures of Central Tendency

* **Mean** – Average value
* **Median** – Middle value
* **Mode** – Most frequent value

---

## - Measures of Dispersion

* **Range** – Difference between maximum and minimum
* **Variance** – Measure of spread
* **Standard Deviation** – Square root of variance
* **Interquartile Range (IQR)** – Spread of middle 50%

---

## 🔹 3. Gaussian Distribution

A symmetric, bell-shaped distribution where most values lie near the mean.

---

## - Empirical Rule (3-Sigma Rule)

* 68% of data lies within 1 standard deviation
* 95% within 2 standard deviations
* 99.7% within 3 standard deviations

---

## - Percentiles & Quartiles
* Percentiles divide data in 100 part
* Q1 (25th percentile)
* Q2 (Median)
* Q3 (75th percentile)

---

## - Five Number Summary

* Minimum
* Q1
* Median
* Q3
* Maximum

---

## - Skewness

* Skewness indicates direction of distribution.
* Positive skew → tail on the right
* Negative skew → tail on the left

---

## - Kurtosis

Measures the peak or flatness of a distribution.
